Background

About NBJC

National Black Justice Coalition, or NBJC, is an American civil rights organization focusing on empowerment of Black lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer+, and same gender loving (LGBTQ+/SGL) people, including people living with HIV/AIDS through coalition building, federal policy change, research, and education.

About the Project

This year, our team worked with NBJC to create a web application that will allow them to reach out to and build a coalition of like-minded organizations with similar focuses, and for public users to access this community and database, as well as for organizations in the network to share resources and spread events.

I worked on our product design and strategy, and with our developers Sonja Johanson, Bryanna Gavino, Gabe Espinosa, Cindy Zhang (FA20), and led by our project leaders Frederick Kim (FA20) and Calvin Chen (SP21).

The problem space

Societal

Organizational

We conducted user research with both NBJC employees and representatives of other partner organizations.

For NBJC, an organization that works both at an advocacy and direct community level, there was no existing platform or technology that was exactly the right solution for their new avenue of community engagement: to have and continuously update a living record of Black, LGBTQ/SGL focused organizations. Their previous and current methods that weren't covering their needs included spreadsheet tools (such as Airtable), Event platforms (Facebook Events, Eventbrite), and NPO specific tools as well, such as Guidestar (a large and extensive database of orgs).

With Airtable and other spreadsheet tools, it was not possible to easily discover organizations, and difficult for NBJC to manually add new organizations to their network. Additionally, as the database grew, it became difficult to manage and effectively utilize the information they collected.

Though NBJC had tried using event specific platforms like Facebook Events and Eventbrite before, those platforms were more suited towards one-off events, rather than learning deeply about a group of organizations and their activities. Additionally, it wasn't a comprehensive BIPOC and Queer listing of organizations.

Lastly, NPO-focused platforms like Guidestar, which is a large and extensive paywalled database of non profit organizations, did not fulfill NBJC's desire to create an interactive and engaging community style platform that would be accessible to the public.

Existing platforms were not the right solutions

  • Airtable / Spreadsheets
    • Not able to easily discover and "add" new organizations
    • Annoying to update manually and keep track of
    • As database grows large, hard to manage and make use of the information
  • Facebook Events
    • Not a comprehensive BIPOC, Queer, listing of orgs
    • More for discovering one-off events, rather than learning about an organization and their activities

  • Eventbrite
    • Similar to Facebook Events
    • Very events focused, but not as strong of a tool for discovering organizations
  • Guidestar
    • Paywalled..
    • Is sort of the main "database" for orgs, but not an interactive, community style platform that would be accessible for the masses.

We wanted to create a solution that encompassed the best parts of these various platforms for NBJC. That meant the solution would have to be easily accesible to the public, flexible, and simple to navigate, customize, and use, while still being able to handle a large amount of information about various organizations. Additionally, it had to give a certain level of control to NBJC staff and moderators to keep the network running smoothly. Of course, building out a custom solution also allowed the platform to be Black LGBTQ+/SGL focused.

Some of our beginning ideas for the whole app and specific features included:

  • A "registry" for organizations to list their information
  • An interactive map specifically tailored for NPOs
  • An events finder for people to discover events
  • A resource center for organizations to share resources with each other.
  • An organization application form and portal

Another aspect we had to consider was different user-facing sides of the application, since NBJC staff would have to moderate, while organizations would need to manage their profiles. Thus, we ended up with three types of users: Organizations, NBJC staff (Moderator and Administrator), and a default, public user.

This led us to also think about specific administration tools for NBJC staff and the ways their actions would interact with Organizations' actions, such as:

  • Approving or rejecting NPO applications to join the network
  • Moderating events that organizations submit
  • Editing the application questions for NPOs
  • Other administrative actions:
    • Resetting passwords
    • Suspending users
    • Inviting moderators and organizations manually

Synthesis

Combining the ideas into one product, we decided what features were essential, and what could be saved for later.

MTP

  • Organizations apply to join the network
  • Moderators from NBJC approve these applications
  • Organizations are onboarded, listing their information, mission, details, location, etc
  • All users can discover organizations with the Map and Search features, by location, categories of organizations, audiences, and more.

MVP

  • Build in an events
  • Later include resource center

Retrospective

Impact on the NPO

Once completed, the web application will be an important tool for supporting backbone of our constituency relations work.

Having a strong network of organizations with this application and technology would let NBJC have a much larger impact with and on other organizations to sound the alarm and work more collectively with entities that focus on their constituency.
